Crime Data Mapped Across the UIUC Campus

Crime on campus has been a growing concern for students, especially since the start of quarantine. We have heard a lot of warnings via word-of-mouth about certain areas and streets on campus that may be potentially dangerous or having an increase of crime. We wanted to visualize crime data from the University of Illinois Police Department's official crime records against a map of campus to help inform students about what areas would be safer or "unsafe." We hope this will be especially helpful for new students who are more unfamiliar with campus, as well as perhaps for the UIPD itself.

In particular, we want to analyze what parts of campus could be considered crime "hot spots" or have higher frequencies or incidents, as well as what types of crimes are more common on campus areas versus off-campus areas. We also want to examine the frequency of crime during specific time periods, such as weekdays or weekends.

While all the utilized data is public information, we recognize possible ethical concerns with this visualization such as alerting users to high traffic police presences or places of low patrol. We also realize that our dataset is limited and could be skewed by certain locations or repeat offenders so we ask that this visualization be used for exploration only.
